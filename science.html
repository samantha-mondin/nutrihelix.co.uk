Why is genetics important for nutrition?


Genetics plays a crucial role in nutrition as it can impact how individuals metabolise and utilise various nutrients, ultimately affecting their overall health and disease risk. Research shows that a one-size-fits-all approach to nutrition advice is often ineffective due to the significant individual differences among people, mainly explained by their unique DNA.
Genetic variations can impact an individual's ability to absorb certain nutrients, their sensitivity to specific food components, and their overall response to dietary interventions. Understanding an individual's unique genetic makeup can provide important insights into how an individual's unique genetic makeup may influence their response to different nutrients and dietary components, in this way identifying areas of nutrient deficiencies, requirements, food intolerances, and disease allowing for more targeted and personalised nutrition recommendations that can optimise individualâ€™s health.

What is the difference between nutrigenomics and nutrigenetics?


Nutrigenomics and nutrigenetics are both scientific fields that focus on the relationship between nutrition and genes. However, while nutrigenomics studies how nutrition can affect your genes expression, nutrigenetics studies how your DNA can impact your body response to nutrients and diet.

How can nutrigenetics help me improve my health?


Nutrigenetics can be used in several ways, such as:

•  Providing valuable insights into how your genes and diet interact to affect your health and influence your disease risk, in this way helping to identify ways to prevent or manage chronic diseases such as cardiovascular disease, diabetes, cancer and obesity.

•  Helping to identify your specific nutritional needs and deficiencies, providing personalised nutrition recommendations that are tailored to your individual needs.

•  Identifying how your genes influence your food preferences and dietary choices.

•  Identifying genetic variations that may make you more susceptible to certain food intolerances or allergies, helping you to make informed dietary choices.

•  Helping athletes to optimise their training and nutrition to improve performance and reduce the risk of injury.

What kind of genetic testing is involved in nutrigenetics?


The genetic testing involved in nutrigenomics typically involves analysing an individual's DNA to identify specific genetic variations that may influence how their body processes and responds to different nutrients and dietary components. DNA is generally collected and extracted from a saliva sample and then analysed using various genetic testing method such as real time-PCR, genotyping arrays and DNA sequencing.

What kind of information can I expect to receive from a nutrigenetics test?


A nutrigenomics test can provide information about your genetic variants related to nutrient metabolism, nutrient absorption, food sensitivities and food preferences. Based on this information, you can receive personalised recommendations for diet, exercise, and lifestyle modifications.

Is nutrigenetics testing covered by insurance?


Nutrigenomics testing is not typically covered by insurance, as it is considered to be a non-medical or elective form of genetic testing. This type of testing is often performed by private companies or research institutions, and individuals usually have to pay for the test themselves. However, some companies may offer payment plans to help make testing more accessible.

Is nutrigenetics testing accurate?


Nutrigenetics testing can provide valuable insights into how an individual's genes and diet interact to affect their health, but there are limitations to its accuracy due to the complexity of relationships between genetics and nutrition/health. While some genetic variations have been well-studied and have established associations with specific health outcomes or nutritional needs, other genetic variations have more complex or uncertain relationships with nutrition and health.

This is because many other factors beyond genetics (such as lifestyle, environment, and microbiome) can influence individual's body responds to different nutrients and dietary components. However, despite these limitations, nutrigenomics testing can still provide very important and useful insights that can allow the development of personalised nutrition recommendations than can help you optimising your health.

This is why, to overcome these limitations, when pursuing nutrigenomics testing it's very important to rely on reputable testing provider and specialised healthcare professional or genetic counsellor that can help you to interpret understand your DNA test results in the context of other health and lifestyle factors. They will help you to understand the potential benefits and limitations of this type of testing at the same time ensuring the accuracy and reliability of nutrigenomics testing.

Can nutrigenetics testing tell me if I am at risk for certain diseases?


Genetic tests in nutrition analyze only variations in modifier/metabolic genes, which are involved in the metabolism and processing of nutrients and dietary components. These tests do not analyse disease risk genes that can predict specific disease outcomes or if an individual is at risk for a particular disease.

However, since diet and nutrition represent an important modifiable risk factor that can generally influence the risk of developing several chronic diseases such as CVD, obesity, diabetes, and cancer, genetic tests in nutrition can provide valuable information on how the interaction between our genes and diet can influence and modify our general disease risk.

It is also important to note that genetics and diet are just some of the factors that contributes to disease risk, and other lifestyle and environmental factors such exercise also play a role. Therefore, nutrigenomics testing cannot be considered a predictor of disease outcomes. However, it can be used to develop a comprehensive plan for disease prevention and management based on an individual's needs and risk factors.

How does genotype-based nutrition counselling differ from traditional nutrition counselling?


Genotype-based nutrition counselling uses genetic testing, as well as other information such as age, gender, blood biomarkers, health status, physical activity level, food preferences, and body composition to provide personalised recommendations for nutrition and lifestyle modifications.

This approach achieves a higher degree of personalisation and precision in the advice offered, making genotype-based personalised nutrition useful for developing targeted prevention strategies through dietary intervention.

In contrast, traditional nutrition counselling typically relies on general dietary recommendations that do not take into account an individual's unique genetic makeup.

In addition, genotype-based nutrition counselling involves analysing complex genetic data and therefore requires specialised training and expertise to interpret and apply the results effectively.

This report may include personalised nutrition recommendations based on your genetic profile.
